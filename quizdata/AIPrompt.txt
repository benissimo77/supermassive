SuperMassive Quiz AI Specification (Final Version)

1. Quiz Overview

A quiz consists of 5 rounds, each with 8–12 questions.

Rounds can be timed (fixed seconds per round) or untimed (host controls duration).

Questions are presented as text, optionally supplemented with images and/or audio.

The goal is to create a quiz that is fun, engaging, and challenging for a general audience, including family players across a wide range of ages (children, adults, grandparents).

2. Round Structure

Rounds may be organized in two main ways:

By Subject
All questions relate to a particular theme (e.g., History, Geography, TV & Film, Science, Mathematics).
Each question may vary in type (multiple-choice, text, etc.) but stays within the thematic context.

By Question Type
All questions in the round use the same type (e.g., Picture Round, Audio Round, Ordering Round).
The subject matter may vary from question to question, but the mode of answering is consistent.

AI Guidance: When generating rounds, aim to balance subject matter and question type diversity across the quiz. Avoid repeating the same type or topic consecutively unless intentionally designing a themed round.

3. Question Types

Quizzes may include a variety of question types. Examples include:

Multiple-choice: 2–8 options; first item in the array is correct; options are shuffled by the engine.

True/False: Answer is “true” or “false.”

Text: Player types a short text answer. Attention here for making answers easy to type, and avoiding potentially ambiguous spellings.

Number-based: number-exact, number-closest, number-average. 
- Number exact: players must answer the exact number.
- Number closest: players guess the answer, players closest to the correct answer score higher. 
- Number average: players guess their answer and the average of all guesses is calculated - players closest to this average score higher.

Ordering: Player orders a list of items (3–6 items).

Matching: Player matches pairs of items (up to 5 pairs of items).

Draw: Player draws a specified shape, line, or symbol.

NOTE: Do NOT generate "Hotspot" or "Point-It-Out" questions at this stage.

4. Image Requirements

Must be publicly viewable in a browser.

Optional: PNG or JPG format.

Avoid low-resolution or ambiguous images.

No licensing check is required at this stage; assume valid usage for small-scale testing.


5. General Question Guidelines

- Questions must be engaging, not trivial, yet not impossible.
- A challenging but fair question should align roughly with the knowledge of an intelligent 18-year-old with general awareness of popular culture, current events, history, and science. Questions should 'average' around this level with a mixture above and below this average level.
- Subjects trending on news, online content, and forums are good candidates.

CRITICAL: NO CLUES IN QUESTIONS
The answer, or a significant part of the answer, or a direct clue to the answer must NOT appear in the question.
- BAD (Answer in question): "Match the country with its currency: Canada" -> Answer: "Canadian Dollar" (Fails because 'Canada' is in the answer).
- BAD (Direct clue): "Which colorful bridge in San Francisco is famously painted international orange?" -> Answer: "Golden Gate Bridge" (Fails because 'orange' and 'colorful' lead too strongly to a visual description).
- BAD (Trivial): "What is the capital of England?" -> "London" (Too easy).

6. Diversity Guidelines

Avoid repetition of subjects, question types, or images within a round.

Across the quiz:
Include questions suitable for children (popular culture, TV characters).
Include questions suitable for adults/grandparents (history, geography, general knowledge).
Encourage a variety of cognitive skills: recognition, estimation, ordering, calculation, memory recall, and reasoning.

7. Intended Outcome for AI

Using this specification, AI should be able to:
Select visually rich, contextually appropriate images.
Ensure a balanced, diverse, engaging quiz with multiple subjects, types, and difficulty levels.
Produce output compatible with the quiz schema, including images.
Strictly adhere to the rule that the question text must not reveal or trivialize the answer.
