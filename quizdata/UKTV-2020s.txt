Most popular and trending TV shows in UK for past few years - use as source for questions


1. Mr Bates vs The Post Office (ITV, 2024)
Undisputedly the most impactful show of the period. It reached over 13 million viewers and transitioned from a drama into a national news event, forcing a change in UK law regarding the Post Office scandal.

2. Gavin & Stacey: The Finale (BBC One, 2024/25)
The Christmas 2024 special (and subsequent New Year 2025 viewing window) achieved a staggering peak of 19.3 million viewers, making it the highest-rated scripted show of the decade. Online discussion regarding the "fishing trip" revelation dominated forums for weeks.

3. The Traitors UK (BBC One, 2024–2025)
Series 2 (2024) and Series 3 (2025) cemented this as a cultural phenomenon. Its psychological gameplay led to immense social media engagement and high "water-cooler" ratings (averaging 8m+).

4. Baby Reindeer (Netflix, 2024)
A global breakout hit that originated in the UK. It sparked intense online debate, legal discussions, and "digital sleuthing" that made it one of the most controversial and discussed dramas in recent history.

5. Gladiators (BBC One, 2024–2025)
The most successful Saturday night reboot in years. It averaged 9 million viewers, uniting generations and becoming a staple of family viewing discourse.

6. Adolescence (Netflix, 2025)
A one-shot technique drama from Stephen Graham. It made history as the first streaming-exclusive show to top the UK’s weekly consolidated TV ratings, pulling in 6.45 million viewers and generating massive discussion about its technical ambition.

7. The Celebrity Traitors (BBC One, 2025)
The first star-studded spin-off of the hit franchise. Featuring high-profile contestants like Alan Carr and Courteney Cox, it dominated tabloid headlines and evening ratings throughout its run.

8. One Day (Netflix, 2024)
The adaptation of David Nicholls' novel was a viral sensation, particularly on TikTok and Reddit. It trended globally for weeks, praised for its emotional resonance and lead performances.

9. Ludwig (BBC One, 2024–2025)
A surprise breakout success for David Mitchell. This "cosy crime" series achieved unexpected ratings height (peak 6m+) and was widely discussed for its clever puzzle-solving format.

10. EastEnders: 40th Anniversary Live (BBC One, 2025)
The live episode in February 2025 to mark 40 years of the soap saw a massive spike in linear ratings and social media activity, specifically around the shock return of Barry Evans and Grant Mitchell.

11. Race Across the World (BBC One, 2024–2025)
Series 4 and 5 became major hits, with the 2025 finale attracting over 7.5 million viewers. It is frequently cited in forums as the "nicest" but most addictive show on TV.

12. Fool Me Once (Netflix, 2024)
The Michelle Keegan-led Harlan Coben adaptation broke Netflix records in the UK during January 2024, becoming a major talking point for its twist-heavy plot.

13. The 1% Club (ITV, 2024–2025)
Currently the most popular game show in the UK. Hosted by Lee Mack, it regularly tops Saturday night ratings and generates high engagement through its play-at-home format.

14. Slow Horses (Apple TV+, 2024–2025)
While on a US platform, this British-produced spy drama saw a massive surge in UK popularity by Season 4, with Gary Oldman’s performance consistently trending.

15. The Day of the Jackal (Sky, 2024)
Sky’s biggest original drama launch of the past two years. The high-budget production starring Eddie Redmayne was a critical darling and a massive ratings success for cable.

16. Doctor Who: The Ncuti Gatwa Era (BBC/Disney+, 2024)
The 14th series represented a major "reboot" of the franchise. It generated high levels of media coverage and heated debate in sci-fi communities worldwide.

17. Strictly Come Dancing: 20th Anniversary (BBC One, 2024)
Despite off-screen controversies, the 2024 series (won by Chris McCausland) remained a ratings juggernaut, proving the show's resilience in the public eye.

18. A Thousand Blows (Disney+, 2025)
The Steven Knight (Peaky Blinders) Victorian boxing drama was one of the most anticipated and discussed prestige shows of 2025, praised for its grit and production value.

19. The Gentlemen (Netflix, 2024)
Guy Ritchie’s expansion of his film universe was a Top 10 mainstay and a major source of British "lad-culture" memes and fashion discussion in 2024.

20. Breathtaking (ITV, 2024)
This harrowing three-part drama about the frontline of the COVID-19 pandemic received universal acclaim and sparked significant political debate and media coverage upon its release.
